This project, "From Votes to Value: Restaurant Analytics", investigates customer behavior and service performance trends within the restaurant industry using structured data and analytical techniques. The primary objective is to understand how service features—such as online ordering, table booking, pricing, and restaurant type—impact customer engagement and perceived success.
The dataset consists of 148 restaurant entries with attributes including ratings, votes, online service availability, cost for two, and service categories (e.g., Café, Buffet, Quick Bites). Data cleaning, transformation, and exploratory data analysis (EDA) were performed using Python libraries like Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn. Key visualizations and Tableau dashboards highlighted customer preferences, pricing distributions, and service usage patterns.
A predictive model using Random Forest Classifier was developed to evaluate the success likelihood of restaurants offering only online ordering. Success was defined by high ratings and substantial customer votes. The model achieved an accuracy of 73%, revealing that customer engagement (votes, ratings), pricing, and restaurant type are the most influential factors in determining success. The findings suggest that online presence alone is insufficient; a balanced mix of digital accessibility, affordability, and customer satisfaction drives restaurant profitability in competitive markets.
